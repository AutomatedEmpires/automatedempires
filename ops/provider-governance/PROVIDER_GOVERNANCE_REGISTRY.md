# Provider Governance Registry

**Implementation snapshot:** 2026-07-12
**Authority:** Canonical non-secret governance register derived from the five Agent 3 account-governance reports
**Scope:** Provider ownership, billing, recovery, MFA, venture mapping, shared-account risk, transfer risk, and required governance work
**Operating mode:** Documentation only; no provider state was changed

## How to use this registry

This is the implementation baseline for provider governance, not a new audit and not a statement of rollout readiness. A project, repository, folder, application, token label, or account name is a useful operational boundary, but it is not venture ownership unless billing, privileged administration, recovery, and a provider-supported recipient acceptance path move with it.

Every material change to a provider should update the affected row with a dated, non-secret evidence reference. Do not record secret values, private recovery destinations, personal contact details, payment details, private account identifiers, or customer identities here.

Status vocabulary is deliberately limited to:

- **known** — established by the 2026-07-12 authenticated observations, explicit founder facts, or identified historical evidence described in the cell;
- **unknown** — not established; never interpret this as absent, disabled, free, or safe;
- **inferred** — supported by resource names or configuration, but not confirmed by the authoritative account surface;
- **blocked** — verification requires re-authentication, working credentials, a missing provider/CLI surface, or billing-only access;
- **needs founder** — a choice, approval, legal decision, payment decision, or privileged manual action is required.

## Rollout gates versus transfer/sale gates

| Gate classification | Governance fixes required before the gate | Current classification |
|---|---|---|
| **Before rollout — operating-control gate** | For every provider a venture will actually use: verified primary and backup administration; MFA and recovery evidence; least-privilege human and machine access; current billing owner, alerts, caps, and renewal posture; working domain/mail/runtime ownership; backup/restore and incident paths; and founder/legal approval of public visibility for each of the 13 GitHub repositories | **Not assessed as passed.** These are governance prerequisites only; product, security, and venture-specific rollout evidence lives outside this registry. Nothing here claims rollout-readiness |
| **Before transfer/sale — ownership-acceptance gate** | Every applicable rollout governance fix, plus documented legal/account/billing/data/domain/payment authority; venture-owned or provider-supported recipient-accepted administration and recovery; complete cross-provider dependencies and retention duties; tested transfer, continuity, and rollback; and a post-transfer access-removal plan | **Not passed.** No venture has complete recipient acceptance, independent ownership/recovery/billing evidence, and a tested transfer rehearsal |

Transfer/sale gates are additive: passing an operating-control gate does not make a venture independently owned or transferable. A venture may also have stricter product-specific rollout blockers that this registry does not evaluate.

## Portfolio implementation rules

1. Keep shared control planes explicitly parent-owned until a founder-approved venture transfer gate exists. Never describe a venture-scoped project as venture-owned solely because of its name.
2. Require two privileged human recovery paths where the provider supports them. The emergency administrator must use MFA and must not depend on the routine operator's device or credential.
3. Record billing, technical, legal, data, domain, and emergency authority separately. One account owner must not be assumed to hold every authority.
4. Keep a names-only inventory of humans, guests, contractors, apps, sessions, tokens, service accounts, delegated access, and their venture/environment scopes.
5. Replace broad machine authority only through create, install, deploy, verify, observe, and separately approve revocation. No registry entry authorizes rotation or deletion.
6. Require a provider-supported recipient invitation or transfer path to be accepted without copying a founder credential.
7. Require billing visibility, recovery evidence, data/backup ownership, rollback, and post-transfer access removal before marking a provider or venture transfer-ready.
8. Treat every unknown and blocked item as open implementation work. No provider or venture is transfer-ready in this snapshot.
