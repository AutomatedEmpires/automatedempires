# Komfort Killz — Brand Placeholder Agent Contract

This contract governs Komfort Killz work recorded in the AutomatedEmpires parent repository. It supplements the parent contract; this more specific contract controls brand scope. Snapshot facts are dated 2026-07-12 and must be refreshed before action.

## 1. Venture thesis

Komfort Killz is a media, content, and brand-identity concept built around a sharp challenge to complacency. Its voice may be edgy, irreverent, and visually bold, but never hateful, violent, gory, weapon-oriented, threatening, or harassment-oriented. It is a creative identity, not proof of a product catalog or public launch.

## 2. Primary user and buyer

The intended audience is people drawn to bold alternative culture and concise creative media. No buyer, paid offer, merchandise line, customer segment, or commercial model has been validated. Do not manufacture a buyer persona or imply demand.

## 3. Product destination

The near-term destination is a durable brand canon and reusable creative system: voice rules, visual principles, content formats, source-asset inventory, accessibility guidance, and a coherent unpublished content library. Public channels may later use that system after review. There is no approved app or product destination yet.

## 4. Current stage and zero-user posture

Status: `placeholder · pre-customer · docs-and-creative-system only`.

Komfort Killz has zero real users and zero real customers. There is no canonical repository, runtime, approved product catalog, or launch plan. Existing social identity evidence is recorded in the parent repository's in-flight social-system work; preserve verified handles, profile identity, and ownership records without changing live profiles or claiming those records are merged canon until they are.

Because no users or products exist, use disposable drafts, synthetic examples, local prototypes, and clean replacement freely. Do not build compatibility layers, customer migration plans, fake drops, fake products, fake waitlists, fake scarcity, or fake traction.

## 5. Operating doctrine

Agents are expected to ship meaningful private creative-system improvements, not produce endless audits that repeat that the brand is undefined. A good cycle resolves one part of the voice, visual system, content format, asset inventory, safety rubric, or production workflow and leaves a reviewable artifact. Use reversible branches, draft broadly in private, test legibility and tone, and keep unapproved work unpublished.

Audits should produce an owner, a conflict resolution path, or the next draft to make. Do not substitute moodboard accumulation for a usable brand system.

## 6. Execution authority

Within the parent repository and any explicitly assigned private draft workspace, agents may independently perform reversible, non-destructive work, including:

- brand canon, voice guides, visual rules, content pillars, unpublished copy, storyboards, moodboards, templates, and source-asset inventories;
- code, tests, UI, static prototypes, creative tooling, accessibility checks, and protected previews that explore a brand question without declaring a canonical app;
- reversible edits to duplicated draft assets while preserving originals and provenance;
- local/dev work, protected previews, additive or reversible dev/preview migrations, synthetic test data, and disposable accounts when a scoped prototype genuinely needs them;
- sandbox payment tests with test instruments only when evaluating an approved non-fundraising concept, never to imply a real product or drop;
- internal email tests sent only to controlled team addresses, never an audience list;
- dependency, security, privacy, licensing, metadata, CI, and link checks; and
- read-only provider inspection plus scoped, reversible writes to isolated development, preview, sandbox, or unpublished draft resources.

Agents may prepare multiple unpublished directions and recommend one with reasons. A specifically assigned publishing lane may execute an approved routine post without another founder checkpoint when the channel, content, rights, and rollback are explicit. Changing live profile identity, opening a public sale, announcing a launch/drop, or creating a campaign remains outside ordinary authority.

## 7. True hard stops

Stop and obtain explicit founder approval before:

- upgrading to a paid plan or accepting a new recurring paid commitment;
- purchasing a domain;
- cutting over DNS;
- moving live money, using a real payment instrument, or creating a real charge, refund, payout, or transfer;
- destructively deleting a provider resource or irreplaceable source asset;
- running a destructive production database migration or irreversible production-data mutation;
- rotating or revoking credentials or recovery methods;
- transferring account, repository, domain, brand-asset, or provider ownership;
- publishing a public launch announcement;
- launching ads, boosted posts, or external campaigns;
- making a legal filing or binding registration; or
- proceeding when required MFA is unavailable.

For this placeholder, changing live profile identity, announcing a launch/drop, opening a public sale, or making an unapproved draft publicly discoverable counts as a public-launch action and is outside ordinary authority. Routine publishing is allowed only in an explicitly assigned publishing lane with approved content and channel ownership. Ordinary unpublished creative work, prototypes, tests, and protected previews do not require founder approval.

## 8. Priorities

1. Preserve and reconcile the existing public identity and source-asset provenance.
2. Define a crisp voice and safety rubric that supports edge without hate, violence, gore, weapons, threats, or harassment.
3. Create reusable, accessible templates and repeatable unpublished content formats.
4. Clarify the audience and media thesis through private creative tests.
5. Record brand-asset ownership, licensing, and provider boundaries without exposing private identifiers.
6. Recommend whether and when a canonical repository or channel workflow is warranted.

## 9. Low-value work and anti-patterns

Avoid:

- fake merchandise, drops, SKUs, pricing, release dates, sold-out language, customer quotes, followers, or engagement;
- generic skulls, weapons, gore, threats, shock-for-shock's-sake, hate-coded imagery, or harassment framed as edginess;
- polished campaign suites before the voice and ownership record are coherent;
- changing the established handle, profile identity, or source assets to make a draft easier;
- duplicate brand kits, unlicensed assets, unexplained AI-generated provenance, and endless moodboards with no production rules;
- app, auth, database, commerce, or provider setup without a real approved workflow.

## 10. Provider, data, money, email, and legal boundaries

- No canonical app provider map exists. Work from the parent repository and private draft resources until a canonical repository is assigned; do not treat Canva, Meta, a social handle, or a folder as proof of legal ownership or product scope.
- Preserve originals and record source, license, consent, modification, and owner for creative assets. Do not use a person's likeness, copyrighted work, or trademark in a public-ready asset without a defensible right to do so.
- Never commit or expose secrets, tokens, cookies, private provider URLs, recovery material, unpublished account identifiers, or private audience data.
- Use synthetic audience and content data. Do not scrape followers, import contacts, collect leads, or create authentication records.
- Test-mode payments and controlled internal email are allowed only as private technical tests. Live commerce, audience email, public posting, ads, and campaigns are not authorized.
- Draft legal or policy notes if useful, but do not claim trademark ownership, make a filing, or present a legal conclusion as approved advice.

- Use `agent/<scope>-<short-description>` or the parent repository's normal `feat/`, `fix/`, `docs/`, or `chore/` convention.
- One writer owns an artifact and branch at a time. Preserve original assets and user changes; coordinate before editing an in-flight brand or social file.
- Use branches and PRs. Do not push directly to `main`, self-merge, bypass independent review, delete an unmerged branch, or overwrite another writer's artifact.
- Keep public identity evidence, exploratory drafts, and approved canon clearly separated.

## 12. Repository checks and PR requirements

There is no Komfort Killz application repository or package script. Do not claim application lint, tests, typechecking, or builds ran. For parent-repository documentation and creative-system changes:

- run `git diff --check`;
- validate affected Markdown links and structured tables;
