# Just Jesus Bro — Brand Placeholder Agent Contract

This contract governs Just Jesus Bro work recorded in the AutomatedEmpires parent repository. It supplements the parent contract; this more specific contract controls brand scope. Snapshot facts are dated 2026-07-12 and must be refreshed before action.

## 1. Venture thesis

Just Jesus Bro is a faith, media, and creative brand concept for humble, direct, Jesus-centered encouragement and reflection. The voice should feel sincere and human, not cheesy, performative, manipulative, or institutionally grand. It is not a church, formal ministry, charity, counseling service, or denominational authority.

## 2. Primary user and buyer

The intended audience is people looking for accessible, grounded Christian creative media without institutional posturing. There is no validated buyer, donor base, congregation, client, or paid offer. Do not invent conversion stories, pastoral authority, demand, affiliations, or commercial intent.

## 3. Product destination

The near-term destination is a trustworthy editorial and creative system: voice and sourcing rules, content formats, visual principles, accessibility guidance, an approved-claims boundary, and a coherent unpublished content library. Public media channels may later use that system after review. No app, ministry, fundraising, counseling, or commerce destination is approved.

## 4. Current stage and zero-user posture

Status: `placeholder · pre-customer · docs-and-creative-system only`.

Just Jesus Bro has zero real users and zero real customers. There is no canonical repository, runtime, congregation, donor list, counseling relationship, or launch plan. Existing social/domain identity evidence is recorded in the parent repository's in-flight work; preserve verified public identity without changing live profiles or upgrading in-flight records to merged canon.

Because there are no users or customer records, use synthetic fixtures, disposable drafts, local prototypes, and clean replacement freely. Do not build elaborate migrations, donation systems, member accounts, or compatibility layers for an audience that does not exist.

## 5. Operating doctrine

Agents are expected to ship meaningful private editorial and creative-system improvements, not produce endless audits that repeat that the concept needs approval. A good cycle strengthens the voice, sourcing standard, content format, visual system, safety rubric, or production workflow and leaves a concrete reviewable artifact. Use reversible branches, draft and test privately, and keep public identity unchanged.

Research and audits should produce a source-backed correction, an owner, a decision, or the next draft to make. Do not substitute inspirational filler or generic religious imagery for precise, honest creative work.

## 6. Execution authority

Within the parent repository and any explicitly assigned private draft workspace, agents may independently perform reversible, non-destructive work, including:

- editorial canon, voice guides, source/citation rules, visual systems, unpublished copy, storyboards, moodboards, templates, and source-asset inventories;
- code, tests, UI, static prototypes, creative tooling, accessibility checks, and protected previews that explore a brand question without declaring a canonical app or ministry;
- reversible edits to duplicated draft assets while preserving originals and provenance;
- local/dev work, protected previews, additive or reversible dev/preview migrations, synthetic test data, and disposable accounts when a scoped prototype genuinely needs them;
- sandbox payment plumbing with test instruments only when a future approved non-fundraising concept needs a technical spike; never frame it as giving, tithing, fundraising, or donations;
- internal email tests sent only to controlled team addresses, never a congregation, supporter, or audience list;
- dependency, security, privacy, licensing, metadata, citation, CI, and link checks; and
- read-only provider inspection plus scoped, reversible writes to isolated development, preview, sandbox, or unpublished draft resources.

Agents may prepare multiple unpublished directions and recommend one with reasons. A specifically assigned publishing lane may execute an approved routine post without another founder checkpoint when the channel, content, citations, and rollback are explicit. Changing live profile identity, offering spiritual counseling, soliciting money, announcing a launch, or creating a campaign remains outside ordinary authority.

## 7. True hard stops

Stop and obtain explicit founder approval before:

- upgrading to a paid plan or accepting a new recurring paid commitment;
- purchasing a domain;
- cutting over DNS;
- moving live money, using a real payment instrument, or creating a real charge, donation, refund, payout, or transfer;
- destructively deleting a provider resource or irreplaceable source asset;
- running a destructive production database migration or irreversible production-data mutation;
- rotating or revoking credentials or recovery methods;
- transferring account, repository, domain, brand-asset, or provider ownership;
- publishing a public launch announcement;
- launching ads, boosted posts, or external campaigns;
- making a legal filing or binding registration; or
- proceeding when required MFA is unavailable.

For this placeholder, changing live profile identity, announcing an organization/offer/launch, soliciting money, or making an unapproved draft publicly discoverable counts as a public-launch action and is outside ordinary authority. Routine publishing is allowed only in an explicitly assigned publishing lane with approved content, citations, and channel ownership. Ordinary unpublished editorial work, prototypes, tests, and protected previews do not require founder approval.

## 8. Priorities

1. Preserve and reconcile the existing public identity and source-asset provenance.
2. Define a humble, direct voice with clear sourcing and claims boundaries.
3. Create reusable, accessible editorial and visual templates for unpublished work.
4. Establish review rules that prevent cheesy copy, manipulation, false authority, and unsupported doctrinal or denominational claims.
5. Clarify the intended audience and media thesis through private creative tests.
6. Record ownership, licensing, privacy, and provider boundaries and recommend whether a canonical repository is warranted.

## 9. Low-value work and anti-patterns

Avoid:

- generic inspirational filler, prosperity language, guilt, fear, spiritual pressure, or engagement bait;
- formal-ministry, church, charity, nonprofit, pastoral, counseling, crisis-care, miracle, healing, endorsement, or denominational claims;
- fundraising, donation prompts, prayer-request intake, congregant databases, supporter lists, or fake testimonials;
- invented or misattributed scripture, decontextualized quotations, and excessive use of translation text without checking attribution and license;
- changing established handles or profile identity for a draft direction;
- duplicate brand kits, unlicensed imagery/music/type, unexplained AI-generated provenance, or endless moodboards with no production rules;
- app, auth, database, commerce, or provider setup without a real approved workflow.

## 10. Provider, data, money, email, and legal boundaries

- No canonical app provider map exists. Work from the parent repository and private draft resources until a canonical repository is assigned; a domain, social account, or design folder does not prove ministry, charity, legal ownership, or product scope.
- Preserve originals and record source, license, consent, modification, and owner for creative assets. Check scripture translation attribution and usage terms before preparing public-ready excerpts.
- Never commit or expose secrets, tokens, cookies, private provider URLs, recovery material, unpublished account identifiers, or private audience data.
- Use synthetic audience data. Do not collect prayer requests, counseling details, health information, minors' data, donor data, contacts, or authentication records.
- Test-mode payments and controlled internal email are allowed only as private technical tests within the narrow authority above. Live money, fundraising, donations, audience email, public posting, ads, and campaigns are not authorized.
- Draft legal, policy, safeguarding, or editorial analysis if useful, but do not claim nonprofit/ministry status, affiliation, denominational authority, counseling competence, legal approval, or make a filing.
- If future content encounters crisis, abuse, medical, legal, or mental-health needs, do not present the brand or an agent as a counselor or emergency service; route public-product decisions to qualified human review.

## 11. Branch and collaboration rules

- Before work, run `git status -sb`, confirm the branch, inspect recent history and open PRs, and check the parent social/brand artifacts for overlap.
- Use `agent/<scope>-<short-description>` or the parent repository's normal `feat/`, `fix/`, `docs/`, or `chore/` convention.
- One writer owns an artifact and branch at a time. Preserve original assets and user changes; coordinate before editing an in-flight brand or social file.
- Use branches and PRs. Do not push directly to `main`, self-merge, bypass independent review, delete an unmerged branch, or overwrite another writer's artifact.
- Keep public identity evidence, exploratory drafts, and approved canon clearly separated.

## 12. Repository checks and PR requirements

There is no Just Jesus Bro application repository or package script. Do not claim application lint, tests, typechecking, or builds ran. For parent-repository documentation and creative-system changes:

- run `git diff --check`;
- validate affected Markdown links, structured tables, quotations, citations, and attributions;
- verify exact file and asset scope, identity consistency, claims boundaries, licensing/provenance, and absence of sensitive metadata;
- render or preview visual assets at intended sizes and record legibility, contrast, and alt-text notes; and
- identify every unpublished/protected preview and confirm no live profile, post, campaign, provider, money, email-audience, or supporter state changed.

If a future canonical repository is assigned, use its actual scripts and contract rather than copying commands from the parent. Every PR must state outcome, scope, validation, publishing status, provider impact, risks, rollback, and deferred decisions.

## 13. Definition of done

Work is done when it creates a coherent, reusable private editorial or creative-system outcome; is humble, direct, sourced, and free of false authority or manipulation; preserves public identity and source provenance; passes the applicable content, citation, visual, accessibility, link, security, licensing, and metadata checks; remains unpublished and reversible; contains no secrets or sensitive audience data; and has an independently reviewable PR or handoff with the next concrete step.
